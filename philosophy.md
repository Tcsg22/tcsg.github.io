## Philosophical Notes and Explorations

Historically, when a discipline is about to start, it is often given the name philosophy -- the acquisition of knowledge. Like science, before it becomes what it is today, it is natural philsophy. Before chemistry, the study of molecular compound is given a more mystical name alchemy. As civilisation advances, philosophy got demystified and evolved into disciplines of systematic investigations over real world subjects, in the unifying lens of the scientific method. The laws of physics, and the laws of evolution in biology, is a unique defining feature of each discipline that cannot be naively be fitted into a grand narrative of the natural philosphy. However, if there is one field that resists 'de-philosophication', it will be the mind-body problem. 

The mind-body problem, in the modern lens, has almost no significance to the scientific method. Since, as far as the method concerns, eventually everything about the mind will become testable and hopefully quantifiable. Since the 20th century, some aspects of it too has been 'de-philosophicated' into solid field such as psychology and neuroscience. Be sure that the author of this page also takes this stance. However, as soon as one starts to think, 'Why is there such experience of I?', the perfect narrative of the scientific method is suddenly, almost unreasonably, cursed with a feeling of unsatisfation. Objective physicalist may argue that such feeling is biochemistry at its finest. Yet, this negligible gap is big enough to fill in multiple radially different and mutually contradictive world views, that fully defines the reality of each individuals in this modern world. This gap is otherwise known as the hard-problem of consciousness.

The philosophy of mind is still a real standing subject today. In my opinion, ultimately the right problem to solve in this discipline is whether there will ever seen a real formulation to the hard-problem. According to the giant of the Integrated information Theory (IIT) Prof. Anil Seth, this is the wrong question to ask, at least for the time being. One should really focus on the 'real' problem of consciousness. In a nutshell, the real problem concerns a pragmatic approach that maps subjective experience into machines read-outs through which a reproducible theory can be formulated. In particular, IIT seeks to bridge this gap by first explaining the unity of self throught the concept of integrated information. However, IIT secretly obscures the question whether subjective experience should be taken as primary source or secondarr source of the formulation. In my opinion, the ability to address this will be a key advancement in the field.

This blog is dedicated to the better understanding of the discipline through explorations. There are a few areas of interest that I would like to share via this platform, to share some unconventional, but existing perspectives on some aspects of philosophy including its content and its history.
