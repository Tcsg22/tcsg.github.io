## Concepts in Topology/Category Theory

During my studies in theoretical physics, we were trained with the standard curriculum such as real/complex analysis, QFT, differential geometry, gauge theory, and so on. Although this gives a solid basis for a theoretical physicists to solve problems, due to the shear size of mathematical science, in hindsight I realise that there are a large trunk of important knowledges that are left untaught and hidden. 

The modularisation of knowledge sometimes can hide connections between discipline, and I certainly feel it this way between topology and category theory. Therefore, it is the intention of this section to include some of the knowledges in topology/category theory that I think will help myself and hopefully others for the better understand of these two disciplines.

## Interesting Math Website

Prof. Baez is a mathematical physicist who works on higher category theory. His explanation of Octonion is self-contained, and with some works, intuitive to understand. His explanation of the Dynkin diagram through the lens of Coxeter diagram also demystifies the characterzation of Lie algebra. Some of my reviews in mathematics may reference his explanation.
